## Overview

The Education Management Information System (EMIS) was developed as part of a UNICEF initiative to enhance educational data infrastructure in Borno, Adamawa, and Yobe states. This project involved an extensive assessment of the existing system, a strategic roadmap for improvement, baseline school census collection, and the creation of a baseline statistics book to inform future decision-making. The outcome is a robust system capable of driving data-informed strategies for education in the region.

## Key Features

- **Current System Assessment**: Comprehensive analysis of the existing EMIS, identifying gaps and opportunities for modernization.
  
![Data Collection in Action](/images/projects/emis/data-collection.jpg)

- **Strategic Plan Development**: Designed a 3-5 year plan focused on EMIS improvement, aligned with regional and national education objectives.
- **Baseline Census**: Conducted a detailed baseline census of schools in the region, collecting critical data points for analysis.
  
![School Statistics Compilation](/images/projects/emis/school-statistics.jpg)

- **Statistics Book**: Compiled a statistics book to provide stakeholders with actionable insights derived from the census.
- **Data Analytics Integration**: Leveraged advanced analytics using Python, R, and Excel for data cleaning, validation, and interpretation.

## Technologies Used

- **Vue.js**: For developing a user-friendly frontend interface.
- **Java**: Backend development for system logic and database interactions.
- **Python & R**: For advanced analytics, data processing, and insights generation.
- **ODK**: Deployed for accurate and scalable data collection during the census.
- **Excel**: Supported manual data cleaning and reporting for ease of interpretation.

## Challenges and Learnings

The project faced challenges in collecting accurate data from remote areas due to logistical constraints and data discrepancies. Utilizing ODK and a strong validation process significantly improved data accuracy. Integrating advanced analytics tools like Python and R helped uncover deeper insights from the collected data, enabling data-driven decision-making.

## Outcome

The EMIS project successfully modernized education data systems in the targeted states. The baseline census and statistics book provided critical insights, while the strategic plan established a clear roadmap for the future. The enhanced EMIS now serves as a foundation for driving education policy and improving outcomes across the region.
